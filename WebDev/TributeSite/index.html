Carl Friedrich Gauss

The Prince of Mathematics

A summary of Gauss's life:

  • 1777 - Gauss is born in Brunswick, Brunswick-Wolfenbuttel
  • 1792-1795 - Gauss studies in the Gymnasium Carolineum in Brunswick
  • 1795-1798 - Gauss is admitted to the University of Gottingen but leaves in his 3rd year
  • 1799-1801 - Gauss submits his PhD dissertation and release his first publication on the theory of Congruence
  • 1805 - Marries Johanna Osthoff, however she dies. Gauss remarries to Minna Waldeck
  • 1812-1818 - Gauss works on elliptic integrals, the arithmetic-geometric mean, and hypergeometric functions
  • 1823 - Gauss recieves the Copenhagen Prize for his work on the Cauchy-Riemann Equations
  • 1831 - Gauss's second wife, Minna, dies.
  • 1838 - Gauss and Wilhelm Weber, a German Physicist develop the first electromagnetic telegraph
  • 1855 - Gauss dies of heart failure on February 23, 1855
